Building an AI model understandable by humans
Professor Xie and his team of research students are seeking to help you better understand how an AI model is able to predict whether online product reviews are spam or genuine. In understanding our model, we hope that you can not only have a higher trust in the correctness of its output, but also adapt some of the algorithm's strategies to spot spam reviews on your own.
The AI model we use is called SpEagle. It is an AI that has been trained on a Yelp dataset to predict the likelihood that a given review is spam or genuine based on factors, or features as we call them, of both the review itself and the user who left the review. For your participation in this survey, you will be asked to decide the relative importance of these features in determining whether a review is spam or not.
The next page will tell you more about the specific features we are interested in and where we think they should be weighted in terms of relative importance. It is more than okay if you disagree, you will have the opportunity to express this later. If you are curious about any specific feature on the next page, you may mouse over it to explain why it is important.
Note that the list of features we examine today is only a small part of all the features included in the model, but they should be the easiest for the average person to understand, which is our main purpose.
